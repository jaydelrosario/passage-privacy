Privacy Policy

Your privacy matters. Here's how Passage handles your data.

1. About Passage

Passage is a mobile app that helps you capture and organize meaningful quotes and notes from the books you read. This policy explains what data we collect, how we use it, and your rights.

2. Information We Collect

Data stored on your device

Passage stores all of your books, notes, and captured images locally on your device using on-device storage. This data never leaves your device unless you explicitly choose to export or share it. We do not have access to your library content.

Camera and photo library

Passage requests access to your device's camera and photo library so you can capture book covers and pages. Photos you take or select are stored locally on your device. We do not upload your photos to any server except as described below for text recognition.

Text recognition (OCR)

When you use the text recognition feature, images of book pages may be sent to OpenAI's API for processing. This is done solely to extract text from your photos. We do not store these images on our servers. OpenAI's use of this data is governed by OpenAI's API data usage policy.

Analytics

We use PostHog, a product analytics service, to collect anonymous usage data such as which features are used, screen views, and general interaction patterns. This helps us understand how to improve the app. Analytics data does not include the content of your books, notes, or photos. You are not personally identified through analytics.

Device information

Through our analytics provider, we may collect basic device information such as device model, operating system version, and app version. This is used solely for debugging and improving app compatibility.

3. What We Do NOT Collect

  • We do not require an account — there is no sign-up, login, or password.
  • We do not collect your name, email address, or any personal contact information (unless you voluntarily email us for support).
  • We do not use advertising or tracking cookies.
  • We do not sell or rent any data to third parties.

4. How We Use Your Information

We use the information described above to:

  • Provide the core app functionality (capturing and organizing book notes).
  • Process text recognition requests via OpenAI's API.
  • Understand app usage patterns to improve features and fix bugs.

5. Third-Party Services

Passage uses the following third-party services:

  • OpenAI API — for text recognition (OCR) from book page images. OpenAI Privacy Policy
  • PostHog — for anonymous product analytics. PostHog Privacy Policy
  • Open Library / Google Books — for fetching book cover images based on title and author. No personal data is sent to these services.

6. Data Storage and Retention

All of your books, notes, and images are stored locally on your device. We do not maintain a cloud backup of your data. If you delete the app, your data is permanently removed from your device.

Anonymous analytics data is retained by our analytics provider for a limited period to help us understand usage trends.

7. Data Export and Deletion

You can export all of your data at any time using the Export feature in Settings. You can delete individual books and notes within the app, or delete all data by uninstalling the app.

8. Security

All communication with third-party services (OpenAI, PostHog) is encrypted in transit using HTTPS. Your local data is protected by your device's built-in security features (passcode, Face ID, etc.).

9. Children's Privacy

Passage is not directed at children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

10. Your Rights

Since Passage stores data locally on your device and does not maintain user accounts, you have full control over your data at all times. You can:

  • Export your data using the in-app export feature.
  • Delete any or all of your data at any time.
  • Uninstall the app to remove all locally stored data.

11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. We will post the updated policy on this page with a revised effective date. Continued use of the app after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
